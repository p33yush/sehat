Frequently Asked Questions

What is Kerala Migrant Health Records system?

The Kerala Migrant Health Records system is a comprehensive digital health management platform designed specifically for migrant workers in Kerala. It provides:

  • Digital Health Records: Complete medical history and health tracking
  • Health Services Access: Easy access to government health centers
  • Emergency Support: 24/7 emergency health services
  • Disease Prevention: Vaccination and health awareness programs
  • Multi-language Support: Services available in multiple languages

This system ensures that migrant workers receive quality healthcare services regardless of their origin or language.

Who can use this health system?

This health system is designed for:

  • Migrant Workers: All migrant workers working in Kerala
  • Family Members: Spouses and children of migrant workers
  • Dependents: Elderly parents and other dependents
  • All Ages: From infants to elderly family members
  • All Nationalities: Workers from any state or country

Registration is free and open to all migrant workers regardless of their employment status, duration of stay, or documentation.

Is this service available in my language?

Yes! The Kerala Migrant Health system provides multi-language support:

  • Website Languages: English, Hindi, Malayalam, and Tamil
  • Mobile App: Available in all major Indian languages
  • Health Centers: Staff trained in multiple languages
  • Emergency Services: 24/7 helpline in multiple languages
  • Documents: Forms and guides available in regional languages
  • Interpreters: On-call interpreter services at major health centers

You can change the language using the language selector at the top of the website.

How do I contact support if I need help?

Multiple support channels are available to help you:

  • Health Helpline: Call 104 for general health queries
  • Emergency: Call 108 for medical emergencies
  • Technical Support: Contact your nearest health center for app/website help
  • Community Health Workers: Visit your local health worker for assistance
  • Health Centers: Visit any Government Health Center for in-person support
  • Mobile App: Use the help section in the Kerala Migrant Health app

All support services are free and available in multiple languages.

What are the benefits of using this system?

Using the Kerala Migrant Health system provides numerous benefits:

  • Free Healthcare: All services are completely free of cost
  • Digital Records: Your health history is safely stored and accessible anywhere
  • Quick Access: Fast access to medical services and emergency care
  • Family Coverage: Health services for your entire family
  • Preventive Care: Regular health checkups and vaccination programs
  • Emergency Support: 24/7 emergency medical services
  • Language Support: Services in your preferred language
  • Health Education: Access to health awareness and education programs

This system ensures you and your family receive the best possible healthcare in Kerala.

How do I register as a migrant worker?

To register as a migrant worker in Kerala's health system, you can:

  • Visit any Government Health Center with valid ID proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, or Passport)
  • Fill out the migrant worker registration form with personal and health details
  • Provide contact information and emergency contact details
  • Submit required documents and receive your unique health ID
  • Access your health records online through the mobile app or website

Registration is free and takes approximately 15-20 minutes to complete.

What documents do I need for registration?

For registration, you need to bring:

  • Identity Proof: Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, Passport, or any government-issued ID
  • Address Proof: Current address in Kerala (rent agreement, employer letter, or local address proof)
  • Employment Details: Employer information, work location, and job details
  • Contact Information: Mobile number and emergency contact details
  • Family Details: Information about family members (if applicable)

All documents should be original or attested copies. Registration staff will help you with the process.

Can I register my family members too?

Yes, you can register your family members during your registration:

  • Spouse and Children: Can be registered under your health ID
  • Dependent Parents: Can be included in family health records
  • Family Health Tracking: Monitor health status of all family members
  • Emergency Contacts: Update family emergency contact information
  • Vaccination Records: Track immunization schedules for children

Family registration ensures comprehensive health coverage for all your dependents.

What health services are available for migrants?

Kerala provides comprehensive health services for migrant workers including:

  • Primary Healthcare: General health checkups, basic treatment, and preventive care
  • Emergency Services: 24/7 emergency medical care and ambulance services
  • Specialized Care: Access to district hospitals and medical colleges
  • Mental Health Support: Counseling and mental health services
  • Maternal & Child Health: Antenatal care, delivery services, and child immunization
  • Disease Prevention: Vaccination programs and health awareness campaigns
  • Health Records: Digital health records management and tracking

All services are provided free of cost at Government Health Centers.

Are there any charges for health services?

All health services are completely free for registered migrant workers:

  • Consultation: Free doctor consultations at all Government Health Centers
  • Medicines: Essential medicines provided free of cost
  • Diagnostic Tests: Basic tests like blood tests, X-rays are free
  • Emergency Care: Ambulance and emergency treatment are free
  • Specialized Treatment: Referral to district hospitals and medical colleges
  • Vaccinations: All immunization programs are free
  • Maternal Care: Antenatal and delivery services are free

No payment is required for any government health services.

How do I find the nearest health center?

You can find the nearest health center through multiple ways:

  • Search by PIN Code: Use the search function on the website with your area PIN code
  • Search by District: Select your district from the dropdown menu
  • Mobile App: Use the location feature in the Kerala Migrant Health app
  • Call Helpline: Contact 104 for assistance in finding nearby centers
  • Ask Locals: Community health workers can guide you to the nearest center
  • Online Map: Interactive map showing all health centers in your area

All health centers provide services in multiple languages for better accessibility.

What should I do in case of health emergency?

In case of a health emergency, follow these steps:

  • Immediate Action: Call 108 for ambulance services or visit the nearest Government Hospital
  • Emergency Contacts: Contact your registered emergency contact person
  • Health ID: Carry your health ID card for quick access to medical history
  • Language Support: Request for interpreter services if needed (available in multiple languages)
  • Follow-up: Ensure your health records are updated with emergency treatment details

Emergency Numbers: 108 (Ambulance), 104 (Health Helpline), 1077 (Disaster Management)

All emergency services are free and available 24/7 for registered migrant workers.

What if I don't have my health ID during emergency?

Don't worry! Emergency services are available even without your health ID:

  • Immediate Treatment: You will receive emergency care regardless of ID status
  • Verification: Hospital staff can verify your registration using your name and mobile number
  • Retroactive Billing: All emergency services remain free for registered migrants
  • Record Update: Your health records will be updated after treatment
  • Language Support: Interpreter services available in multiple languages
  • Family Notification: Your emergency contacts will be informed

Your safety and health are the top priority during emergencies.

How do I report a health emergency for someone else?

You can report health emergencies for others by following these steps:

  • Call Emergency Numbers: Dial 108 for ambulance or 104 for health helpline
  • Provide Information: Give the person's name, location, and nature of emergency
  • Stay with the Person: Remain with them until help arrives
  • Contact Family: Inform their emergency contacts if possible
  • Follow Instructions: Follow the instructions given by emergency operators
  • Update Records: Ensure their health records are updated after treatment

Emergency services are available for all, regardless of registration status.
